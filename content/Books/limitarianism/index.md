# Media Archive Entry: *Limitarianism*

**Date Logged:** March 26, 2026  
**Media Type:** Book  
**Title:** *Limitarianism: The Case Against Extreme Wealth* **Author:** Ingrid Robeyns  

---

### **The Entry**

I’ve been sitting with this idea that there is a point where having "too much" isn't just a private success, but a public harm. Robeyns argues that nobody should have more than a certain amount—not just because it’s "nice" to share, but because extreme wealth is fundamentally incompatible with democracy and a livable planet.

It’s a heavy read in terms of its implications, but the logic is actually pretty simple: if the world has finite resources, one person having a billion units of something means a million people are lacking the basics. It makes you look at "success" through a much more collective lens.

### **Thoughts on the Morality**

What strikes me most is the shift from "how do we help the poor?" to "why do we allow the ultra-rich?" It’s a subtle but massive change in perspective. It moves the conversation away from charity—which always feels optional and hierarchical—toward structural justice. 

The book makes a compelling case that at a certain threshold, money stops being about "comfort" and starts being about "power"—power that none of us actually voted for. It’s making me rethink a lot about social justice work and where the "ceiling" should be, not just the "floor."
